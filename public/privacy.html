Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 29, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Room 117 Architecture & Design LLC (“Room 117,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, and protects information in connection with our internal business-management application (the “App”). The App is a private tool used by Room 117 staff to manage the firm’s architectural-design projects, client records, documents, and billing.

The App is an internal tool for Room 117 staff. It is not a public consumer service. It connects to systems the firm already uses — including the firm’s own QuickBooks Online account — to keep project and billing information in sync.

1. Information we process

In the course of running the firm’s operations, the App processes:

  • Project & client information — job records, project addresses, phase/status, and the contact details of the firm’s clients (name, email, phone, company).
  • Billing & payment records — contract values, invoices, payments, and amounts outstanding.
  • Documents — proposals, building-department letters, and project files stored in the firm’s Google Drive.
  • Field notes — on-site notes, photos, voice memos, and location captured by staff.
  • Staff account information — the email address and authentication identifiers of Room 117 staff who sign in.
  • Accounting data from QuickBooks Online — see Section 2.

2. QuickBooks Online data

With the firm’s authorization, the App connects to Room 117’s QuickBooks Online company to keep job billing synchronized. Specifically, the App may:

  • Read and create customers and invoices that correspond to the firm’s jobs;
  • Read invoice and payment status to reconcile what has been billed and received.

We request only the com.intuit.quickbooks.accounting scope, and we use this data solely to synchronize Room 117’s own job and billing records with the firm’s own QuickBooks company. We do not sell QuickBooks data, use it for advertising, or share it with any third party except the service providers listed in Section 5 that are necessary to operate the App. If the firm disconnects the App from QuickBooks, the App stops accessing QuickBooks data.

3. How we use information

  • To operate the firm’s project, document, and billing workflows;
  • To create and reconcile invoices and payments with QuickBooks;
  • To authenticate staff and secure access to firm data;
  • To maintain, troubleshoot, and improve the App.

We do not use this information for advertising and we do not sell it.

4. How we protect information

  • In transit: all traffic is encrypted using HTTPS/TLS.
  • At rest: data is stored with providers that encrypt data at rest by default.
  • Access control: only authenticated Room 117 staff can access firm data; access is gated on every internal request. Clients of the firm do not access the App’s internal data.
  • Secrets: credentials and API keys are stored as server-side environment variables and are never exposed to the browser or committed to source control.

6. Data retention

We retain business records for as long as needed to run the firm and meet our legal, tax, and accounting obligations. QuickBooks remains the firm’s system of record for accounting data and is governed by Intuit’s own terms and privacy policy.

7. Your choices and rights

Because the App is an internal firm tool, the people whose information appears in it are primarily the firm’s clients and staff. If you believe Room 117 holds information about you and you wish to access, correct, or delete it, contact us using the details below and we will respond consistent with applicable law.

8. Children’s privacy

The App is a business tool and is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect their information.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will revise the effective date above when we do, and material changes will be reflected on this page.
